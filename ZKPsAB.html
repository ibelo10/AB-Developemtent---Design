Understanding Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP)

What are Zero-Knowledge Proofs?

Zero-Knowledge Proofs are cryptographic methods that allow one party (the prover) to prove to another party (the verifier) that a statement is true without revealing any information beyond the validity of the statement itself.

ZKP in Our System

  • Ensures transaction privacy while maintaining auditability
  • Validates transactions without exposing sensitive financial data
  • Enables compliance verification without compromising user privacy
  • Supports selective disclosure for regulatory requirements

Technical Benefits

  • Reduced on-chain data storage requirements
  • Improved scalability through efficient verification
  • Enhanced security through mathematical proofs
  • Compatibility with existing regulatory frameworks
